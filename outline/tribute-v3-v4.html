Leader • Visionary • Father of Modern Vietnam

About President Ho Chi Minh

Ho Chi Minh (born Nguyen Sinh Cung, known as Nguyen Tat Thanh in school, and Nguyen Ai Quoc during his revolutionary activities) was born on May 19, 1890, in Kim Lien village, Nam Dan district, Nghe An province, and passed away on September 2, 1969, in Hanoi.

Ho Chi Minh was born into a patriotic scholarly family and grew up in a region with heroic traditions of resistance against foreign invaders.

Major Achievements

1920

Influenced by the 1917 Russian October Revolution and Lenin's thesis, Nguyen Ai Quoc participated in the 18th Congress of the French Socialist Party, becoming one of the founding members of the French Communist Party.

1941

Convened the 8th Plenum of the Party Central Committee, establishing the Vietnam Independence League (Viet Minh) and organizing armed liberation forces.

1945

On September 2, 1945, at Ba Dinh Square (Hanoi), Ho Chi Minh read the "Declaration of Independence," proclaiming the establishment of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam.

1946

The first National Assembly elected him as President of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam.
